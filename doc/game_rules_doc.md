## Game Overview

**Players**: 2  
**Duration**: ~20 minutes (average 30 rounds, 60 turns)  
**Objective**: Reduce your opponent's Energy to 0 through strategic card play and combat

In Oracle, you command champions from the five Orders of Arcadia, using careful resource management and tactical combat to defeat your opponent. Each turn presents crucial decisions: which champions to deploy, when to draw more cards, and whether to defend against attacks.

---

## Game Components

### The Deck (120 cards total)

**Champion Cards** (102 cards):

- 34 Red champions (Elf, Goblin, Faun, Cyclops, Koatl)
- 34 Indigo champions (Dwarf, Minotaur, Centaur, Fairy, Lycan)
- 34 Orange champions (Human, Orc, Hobbit, Dragon, Aven)

**Draw/Recall Cards** (15 cards):

- 9 cards: Draw 2 cards OR Recall 1 champion (Cost: 1 luna)
- 6 cards: Draw 3 cards OR Recall 2 champions (Cost: 2 lunas)

**Exchange Cards** (3 cards):

- Exchange 1 champion for 5 lunas (Cost: 0 lunas)

### Resources

**Energy** (❤): 

- Starting value: 99 for each player
- Reduces when taking damage in combat
- When a player reaches 0 Energy, they lose

**Lunas** (☾): 

- Starting value: 30 for each player
- Currency used to play cards
- Collect 1 luna at the end of your turn

### Card Attributes

Each champion card has:

- **Cost**: Lunas required to play (0-3)
- **Defense Dice**: d4, d6, d8, d12, or d20
- **Attack Base**: Fixed attack value (0-5)
- **Color**: Red, Indigo, or Orange
- **Species**: One of 15 species (Human, Elf, Dwarf, etc.)
- **Order**: One of 5 mystical orders (A through E)

## Setup

### Deck Distribution

Choose one of three methods:

#### 1. Random Distribution (Recommended for First Games)

- Shuffle all 120 cards together
- Deal 40 cards to each player (players cannot look at cards)
- This is the standard mode with maximum combo variety

#### 2. Monochrome Deck (Balanced Strategy)

- Each player chooses one color (Red, Indigo, or Orange)
- Each player gets:
  - 34 champions of their color
  - 3 "Draw 2" cards
  - 2 "Draw 3" cards
  - 1 Exchange card
- **Total**: 40 cards

#### 3. Custom Deck (Advanced)

- Each player needs their own 120-card set
- Build a 40-card deck with these limits:
  - Maximum 6 "Draw 2" cards
  - Maximum 4 "Draw 3" cards
  - Maximum 4 champions with 0 cost
  - Maximum 2 Exchange cards
- Shuffle your deck before starting

### Initial Setup

1. **Shuffle Decks**: Each player shuffles their 40-card deck
2. **Set Resources**: 
   - Both players start with 99 Energy
   - Both players start with 30 Lunas
3. **Draw Hands**: Each player draws 6 cards
4. **Determine First Player**: Randomly (coin flip, die roll)
5. **Mulligan** (Second player only):
   - May discard up to 2 cards and draw replacements
   - This balances the first player advantage

## How to Win

**Victory Condition**: Reduce your opponent's Energy to 0

**Draw Condition**: If the game reaches 500 turns (250 rounds) with both players still having Energy, the game is a draw (extremely rare).

**Typical Game Length**: 

- Average: 60 turns (30 rounds)
- Range: 24-128 turns

## Turn Structure

Each turn follows this sequence:

### 1. Draw Phase (Beginning of Turn)

- **Active player draws 1 card from their deck**
- **Exception**: First player skips drawing on turn 1
- If your deck is empty when you need to draw, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck

### 2. Action Phase

The active player (attacker) chooses ONE action:

**Option A**: **Attack** - Play 1 to 3 champion cards

- Pay the luna cost for each champion
- Place champions in your combat zone
- Proceed to defense phase

**Option B**: **Play a Draw/Recall Card**

- Pay the luna cost (1 or 2)
- Choose to either:
  - Draw 2 or 3 cards (depending on card), OR
  - Recall 1 or 2 champions from your discard pile to your hand
- Skip to collection phase (no combat)

**Option C**: **Play an Exchange Card**

- No cost to play
- Choose 1 champion from your hand to exchange
- Discard that champion
- Gain 5 lunas
- Skip to collection phase (no combat)

**Option D**: **Pass**

- Do nothing
- Skip to collection phase (no combat)

### 3. Defense Phase (Only if Attack Occurred)

The defending player (opponent) chooses:

**Option A**: **Defend** - Play 0 to 3 champion cards

- Pay the luna cost for each champion
- Place champions in your combat zone

**Option B**: **Decline to Defend**

- Do nothing
- Take full attack damage

### 4. Combat Resolution (Only if Attack Occurred)

- Calculate total attack value
- Calculate total defense value
- Apply damage to defender
- Move all champions from combat zones to discard piles

### 5. Collection Phase (End of Turn)

- Active player collects 1 luna
- Check for game end (defender at 0 Energy?)
- If game continues:
  - Active player discards down to 7 cards (if holding more)
  - Switch active player
  - Begin next turn

## Playing Cards

### Champion Cards

**To Play**:

1. Announce which champions you're playing
2. Pay the total luna cost
3. Place champions in your combat zone

**Limits**:

- Minimum: 1 champion
- Maximum: 3 champions per attack/defense
- Must be able to afford the total cost

**Combat Values**:

- **Attack**: Defense Dice + Attack Base (e.g., d6+2)
- **Defense**: Defense Dice only (e.g., d6+0)

### Draw/Recall Cards

**To Play**:

1. Pay the luna cost (1 or 2)
2. Choose one option:

**Option A: Draw Cards**

- Draw 2 cards (for "Draw 2" card)
- Draw 3 cards (for "Draw 3" card)

**Option B: Recall Champions**

- Return 1 champion from your discard pile to your hand (for "Draw 2" card)
- Return 2 champions from your discard pile to your hand (for "Draw 3" card)
- Champions can only be recalled from discard, not from deck

**Strategic Note**: Drawing gives you more options, but recalling can retrieve powerful champions for future use.

### Exchange Cards

**To Play**:

1. Play the Exchange card (no cost)
2. Choose 1 champion from your hand
3. Discard that champion
4. Gain 5 lunas

**Strategic Note**: Use this when you have expensive champions you can't afford, or need a quick luna boost.

## Combat Resolution

Combat happens when the attacker plays champions and the defender chooses whether to defend.

### Step 1: Calculate Total Attack

```
Total Attack = Sum of (Attack Base + Dice Roll) + Combo Bonus
```

For each attacking champion:

1. Roll their Defense Dice
2. Add their Attack Base
3. Sum all attacking champions
4. Add Combo Bonus (see next section)

**Example**: 

- Champion 1: d6 (rolls 4) + 2 base = 6
- Champion 2: d8 (rolls 7) + 1 base = 8
- Combo Bonus: +5
- **Total Attack: 19**

### Step 2: Calculate Total Defense

```
Total Defense = Sum of (Dice Roll) + Combo Bonus
```

For each defending champion:

1. Roll their Defense Dice
2. **Do NOT add Attack Base** (defense ignores attack value)
3. Sum all defending champions
4. Add Combo Bonus

**Example**:

- Champion 1: d6 (rolls 3)
- Champion 2: d4 (rolls 2)
- Combo Bonus: +7
- **Total Defense: 12**

### Step 3: Apply Damage

```
Damage = MAX(Total Attack - Total Defense, 0)
```

- Subtract Total Defense from Total Attack
- If result is positive, reduce defender's Energy by that amount
- If result is zero or negative, no damage is dealt

**Example**: 19 attack - 12 defense = **7 damage**

- Defender's Energy: 99 → 92

### Step 4: Clean Up

- All champions from both combat zones move to their owner's discard piles
- Combat zone is now empty

## Combo Bonuses

Playing multiple champions with matching attributes grants powerful bonus points. The bonus depends on your deck type and the match.

### Matching Criteria

- **Same Species**: Two or more of the same species (e.g., two Humans)
- **Same Order**: Two or more from the same Order (A-E)
- **Same Color**: Two or more of the same color (Red, Indigo, Orange)

### Random Deck Combos

*Used when decks were distributed randomly*

#### Priority 1: Species Matches (Highest)

- **2 of same species**: +10
- **3 of same species**: +16
- **2 of same species + 1 of same Order**: +14
- **2 of same species + 1 of same Color**: +13

#### Priority 2: Order Matches (If no species match)

- **2 of same Order**: +7
- **3 of same Order**: +11
- **2 of same Order + 1 of same Color**: +9

#### Priority 3: Color Matches (If no species or Order match)

- **2 of same Color**: +5
- **3 of same Color**: +8

**Note**: Only the highest applicable bonus is applied (bonuses don't stack).

### Monochrome Deck Combos

*Used when each player has a single-color deck*

Since all your champions are the same color, color bonuses are removed:

#### Priority 1: Species Matches

- **2 of same species**: +7
- **3 of same species**: +12
- **2 of same species + 1 of same Order**: +9

#### Priority 2: Order Matches (If no species match)

- **2 of same Order**: +4
- **3 of same Order**: +6

### Custom Deck Combos

*Used when players build custom decks*

Same as Monochrome deck bonuses (species, then Order).

## Special Rules

### Mulligan (Second Player Only)

After initial hands are dealt, the second player may:

- Look at their hand
- Choose up to 2 cards to discard
- Draw that many cards to replace them

**Purpose**: Compensates for the first player's advantage of going first.

**Strategy Tip**: Mulligan low-power cards (0-cost d4 champions, cards you can't afford soon).

### Discarding to 7 Cards

At the end of your turn, if you have more than 7 cards in hand:

- You must discard down to exactly 7 cards
- Choose which cards to discard

**Strategy Tip**: Keep high-power champions, affordable cards, and draw/recall cards.

### Empty Deck

When you need to draw a card and your deck is empty:

1. Take your entire discard pile
2. Shuffle it thoroughly
3. Place it face-down as your new deck
4. Draw the top card

**Note**: This can happen multiple times in a long game.

### First Turn Special Rule

The first player (Player A) does NOT draw a card at the start of turn 1. This helps balance the first player advantage.

## Strategy Tips

### Resource Management

**Luna Management**:

- You collect 1 luna per turn (both players)
- Starting 30 + (1 per turn × 30 turns) = 60 lunas available in typical game
- Don't hoard lunas! Unspent lunas at game end are wasted
- Plan 2-3 turns ahead for expensive champions

**Hand Management**:

- Keep 4-7 cards in hand for flexibility
- Discard excess cards strategically (keep powerful/versatile cards)
- Use draw cards when hand is small (< 4 cards)

### When to Attack

**Aggressive Strategy**:

- Play multiple champions early to pressure opponent
- Force opponent to spend lunas on defense
- Risk: Depletes your hand quickly

**Conservative Strategy**:

- Play 1-2 champions per turn
- Maintain hand size with draw cards
- Build up lunas for powerful late-game champions

### When to Defend

**Consider defending when**:

- Attack is very large (> 15 damage)
- You have affordable defenders in hand
- You can make a good combo while defending
- Your Energy is low (< 30)

**Consider declining when**:

- Attack is small (< 8 damage)
- Defenders are expensive or weak
- You're saving lunas for your next attack
- Your Energy is high (> 70)

### Combo Strategy

**In Random Decks**:

- Look for species pairs (strongest combos)
- Save champions of same species for big attacks
- Use single champions early, pairs/triplets later

**In Monochrome Decks**:

- Focus on collecting same-species champions
- Order combos are easier (you have 6-7 of each Order)
- Balanced distribution makes consistency higher

**General Combo Tips**:

- Playing 2-3 low-cost champions with combo often beats 1 high-cost champion
- Combo bonuses apply to both attack AND defense
- A d4+0 champion is valuable if it completes a combo

### Card Value Assessment

**High Priority**:

- Champions with good cost-to-power ratio (d8+1 for 1 luna)
- Champions that match your hand for combos
- Draw cards when hand is small

**Medium Priority**:

- High-cost champions (save for late game)
- Champions that don't match your hand

**Low Priority**:

- Champions you can't afford soon
- Duplicate species when you already have 2-3

### Timing

**Early Game** (Turns 1-10):

- Focus on board presence with affordable champions
- Use draw cards to find good champions
- Build up luna reserves

**Mid Game** (Turns 11-25):

- Deploy 2-champion combos regularly
- Balance attacking and defending
- Start using 2-cost champions

**Late Game** (Turns 26+):

- Use 3-champion combos with high-cost cards
- Calculated aggression (all-in attacks)
- Watch opponent's Energy carefully

### Common Mistakes

❌ **Hoarding lunas**: Spend them! Unspent lunas don't help you win.

❌ **Overdefending**: Taking 5-7 damage is often better than spending 3 lunas.

❌ **Ignoring combos**: A +10 bonus can swing combat dramatically.

❌ **No hand management**: Always keep 4+ cards for options.

❌ **Wrong timing**: Save big attacks for when opponent is low on lunas.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I play more than 3 champions in combat?**  
A: No, maximum 3 champions per attack or defense.

**Q: Do I have to defend?**  
A: No, you can always choose to decline and take full damage.

**Q: Can I recall champions from my deck?**  
A: No, only from your discard pile. Champions in your deck are unknown.

**Q: What happens if I can't discard to 7 cards?**  
A: If you have 7 or fewer cards, do nothing. Only discard if you have 8+ cards.

**Q: Can I play a Draw card and then attack with those drawn cards?**  
A: No, you only get one action per turn. Playing a Draw card ends your action phase.

**Q: Do combo bonuses stack?**  
A: No, only the highest applicable combo bonus is used.

**Q: Can I attack with 1 champion and defend with 3?**  
A: Yes, attacker and defender choose independently how many champions to play (1-3).

**Q: What if both players run out of cards completely?**  
A: This should never happen with proper shuffling of discard pile when deck empties.

**Q: Does the Exchange card have to be used immediately?**  
A: You choose when to play it, but once played, you must exchange a champion immediately.

**Q: Can I recall the champion I just exchanged?**  
A: Not on the same turn, but on future turns yes (it's in your discard pile).

## Glossary

**Active Player**: The player whose turn it is (the attacker).

**Attack Base**: The fixed attack value on a champion card.

**Champion**: A character card that can attack or defend.

**Combo Bonus**: Additional points gained by playing matching champions.

**Combat Zone**: The area where played champions are placed during combat.

**Defense Dice**: The die rolled for each champion (d4, d6, d8, d12, d20).

**Discard Pile**: Face-up pile of used cards visible to both players.

**Energy**: Health points; reaching 0 means defeat.

**Luna**: The game's currency used to play cards (☾ symbol).

**Mulligan**: Second player's option to redraw up to 2 cards at start.

**Order**: One of five mystical groups (A-E) that champions belong to.

**Recall**: Returning a champion from discard pile to hand.

**Species**: The type of champion (Human, Elf, Dwarf, etc.).
